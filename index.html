Many banks, particularly in asia, offer recurrent deposits accounts. Customers have to deposit a fixed amount monthly to the account for a fixed period of time and they'll receive a predefined sum at the end. They've become popular as they suits very well for the people with fixed monthly income. These accounts are marketed under various names and often the effective interest rate is hidden. Usually, the customer will only be given the information on monthly ammount they have to pay and the ammount they receive at the end. The real interest rate is hidden. Unlike with fixed deposit or bonds, for average retail investor, it is quite complex to calculate the underlying interest rate. This calculator helps you to identify the real annual rate of return for any Recurrent Deposit scheme.
